DESCRIPTIVE PARAGRAPH FOR DFD
================================================================================

Patient interactions begin at P1 (Authentication & User Management), where patients and hospital staff input their login credentials, which are verified against D1 (User Accounts) and authentication tokens are generated. Authenticated patients proceed to P2 (Image Upload & Validation), where the NSFW filter validates uploaded skin lesion images by checking skin content percentage (minimum 25%), detecting text or graphics (poster rejection), and screening explicit content before storing validated images in D4 (Image Storage). The validated image data flows to P3 (AI Analysis & Risk Assessment), where the Vision Transformer AI model (external entity) classifies the lesion into seven cancer types with confidence scores, while simultaneously retrieving patient health profiles from D2 (Patient Profiles) including age, skin type (Fitzpatrick scale), family history, and symptom responses collected through a four-step wizard (body location, sensations, visual changes, duration). The AI predictions and symptom data are processed to calculate risk levels (low, medium, high, urgent) with conservative bias favoring false positives, and complete analysis reports are stored in D3 (Analysis Reports) containing cancer classification, confidence scores, risk justification, and recommended consultation timelines. Patients who require medical consultation proceed to P4 (Hospital Search & Navigation), which queries the Google Maps API (external entity) to search dermatology specialists and general hospitals within 50km radius, caching results in D6 (Hospitals) sorted by rating (4.5+ preferred) and distance, then returning hospital lists with directions and contact information to patients. When patients select a facility, data flows to P5 (Appointment Management) where appointment requests are created linking patient_id, hospital_id, and optionally report_id, storing records in D5 (Appointments) with status tracking (pending, confirmed, completed, cancelled) while hospital staff access pending reports from D3 and patient medical history from D2 with privacy protection until appointment confirmation. After staff confirmation or rejection, P5 triggers P6 (Notification Service) which queues emails in D7 (Email Queue) and sends confirmation, rejection, or reminder messages via Email System SMTP (external entity) to both patients and hospital staff. Throughout all system operations, every user action, privacy access, authentication attempt, image validation, AI analysis, appointment booking, and email delivery is logged by P7 (Audit & Logging) into D8 (Audit Logs & Monitoring), creating comprehensive audit trails for security compliance (HIPAA), privacy regulations (GDPR), and forensic investigation capabilities.
